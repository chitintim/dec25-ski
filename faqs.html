Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers!

For First-Timers

Why not just go to Bulgaria/Andorra/somewhere cheap?

Fair question! Tim tried that once. The lifts broke down, the snow was slush, and the "luxury" chalet had a view of a car park. Now he's spent 20+ weeks in Courchevel and refuses to ski anywhere else. Yes, it's pricier, but you get: actual snow, lifts that work, and a hot tub that doesn't smell weird. Your call!

I can't ski. Like, at all. Help?

Perfect! Half the group usually can't ski either. Day 1: You'll fall over. A lot. Day 2: You'll fall over slightly less. Day 3: You'll make it down a green run and feel like a hero. By the end of the week, you'll be racing others to the bar. We book lessons with English-speaking instructors who are used to terrified beginners.

Can't I just book this myself on Booking.com?

Sure, if you enjoy: comparing 47 different transfer companies, working out which ski pass covers what, finding a chalet for exactly 12 people, discovering the "ski-in/out" chalet is actually a 20-minute bus ride away... Tim's already made all these mistakes so you don't have to. Plus he knows which day to ski where based on weather/crowds/hangovers.

The Practical Stuff

What's included in the accommodation?

The chalet comes with all bedding, towels, bathrobes, mid-week cleaning, end-of-stay cleaning, WiFi, and full use of the hot tub and sauna. The kitchen is fully equipped for self-catering, though most evenings we eat out together.

Can I join for part of the week?

Unfortunately not - the chalet is booked for the full week and costs are split equally among all participants. It also helps with group dynamics when everyone's there for the full experience.

How does room allocation work?

We have 6 bedrooms (2 doubles, 1 twin, 3 TBC). Couples get priority for doubles, then it's first-come-first-served. Everyone gets a proper bed - no sofa beds or floor mattresses.

What about dietary requirements?

La Tania has good restaurants that cater to most dietary needs. When we book group meals, we always check requirements. The chalet has a full kitchen if you need to prepare specific meals.

For Returning Skiers

Does Tim actually know secret spots or is he making it up?

20+ weeks = a lot of exploring. He knows: the tree run that ALWAYS has powder two days after a dump, the restaurant with the secret local menu (half price, twice as good), which lift to hit at 11:17am when everyone's at lunch, and that weird cut-through that saves 20 minutes getting back. He's basically a snow nerd at this point.

What if I'm actually good at skiing?

Brilliant! While the beginners are learning to pizza, you can explore: Saulire couloirs (proper steep stuff), Val Thorens glacier (highest skiing in Europe), epic off-piste through the trees, or just blast around showing off. Tim knows where the good stuff is, and there's always someone keen for "just one more run" at 4pm.

Is the hot tub actually that good?

Picture this: It's 5pm. Your legs are jelly. There's snow falling. You're in a hot tub with a beer, looking at mountains, planning tomorrow's routes, and someone's telling a story about their spectacular wipeout. Yes. It's that good. It's basically 40% of why we book this specific chalet.
